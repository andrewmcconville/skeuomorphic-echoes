Skeuomorphic Echoes

From Atoms to Pixels

You will design an interactive Figma experience where users explore a collection of skeuomorphs and how they evolved over time.

 

Example Skeuomorphs

  • The image of a floppy disk as a save icon
  • The image of a paper envelope as an email icon
  • The act of "hanging up" a phone after a call
  • The sound of a camera shutter when taking a picture/screenshot
  • The word "channel" in chat apps or YouTube
  • Many more: p2.0: Example Skeuomorphs

These are skeuomorphs: design elements that reference the form, sound, language, or behavior of an older technology.

They are echoes: residual traces of hand-powered, mechanical, and electronic tools that persist in our virtual interfaces.

They exist to help us understand how something works. Sometimes they provide insight through familiarity. Sometimes they simply linger because no one has imagined a better alternative.

In this project, you will choose four skeuomorphs and trace the evolution of one of your skeuomorphs across time from its:

  • virtual form (current simulated experience)
  • digital stage
  • electric stage
  • mechanical stage
  • low-tech stage

(you may use these stages more than once, not at all, or add your own)

 

Example Evolutionary Chain

The Onscreen Keyboard

  1. (virtual) Smartphone keyboard graphic with haptic key-click sound and vibration
  2. (digital) Computer keyboard buttons and switches
  3. (electric) Electric typewriter, motor-powered hammers on ribbon
  4. (mechanical) Typewriter hammer striking an inked ribbon
  5. (low-tech) Steel nib pen on paper
  6. (low-tech) Quill scratching on parchment

(In this example I tried to include many stages across time, your stages may vary.)

 

Requirements

Each graded assignment will have requirements specific to its deliverable.

  1. Choose four skeuomorphs for global navigation
    • 1 primary skeuomorph: this gets the full evolutionary chain
    • 3 secondary skeuomorphs: each a single page that previews its evolutionary chain
    • Design secondary skeuomorph pages as incomplete previews using appropriate language to your UX pattern such as...
      "release date: 0/0/0000", "coming soon," "purchasable DLC," "level locked," "unexplored area," "pro-tier only," "lost torn-out pages"
  2. For your primary skeuomorph, create an evolutionary chain connecting it to the past (vertical slice, local navigation)
    • Minimum of 3 evolutionary stages (in addition to the primary skeuomorph page)
    • Each evolutionary stage must be more than a line of text (page/screen/view/sidebar/large modal/popup/etc.).
  3. Choose a UX pattern
    • Children's book
    • Choose your own adventure narrative
    • Museum information kiosk
    • Wayfinding map
    • Scavenger hunt
    • Game interface
    • Knowledge/tech tree
    • Timeline
    • Automotive interface
    • Musical instrument
    • Interactive art experience
    • Other (subject to instructor approval)

 

Required Pages

A complete project should have a minimum of 10 pages

  • 1 Home/welcome/landing/start/login page
  • 4 Skeuomorph pages
    • 1 primary (e.g., onscreen keyboard)
    • 3 secondary
  • 3 Evolutionary stage pages (past stages of the primary skeuomorph)
    • (e.g., 1. laptop keyboard → 2. typewriter → 3. pen and paper)
  • 1 List page
    • (e.g., achievements, badges, collectibles, cart, wish list, inventory, completed quests, bookmarks)
  • 1 About/overview/sources page
