Deliverables

  1. Make a new Figma File with 3 pages
    1. Research
    2. Lo-fi Wireframe (used in later assignments)
    3. Hi-fi Prototype (used in later assignments)
  2. Post a link to your Figma file (use the blue share button and copy link button).
  3. Post a screenshot of your Figma file

 

Requirements

Submit a preliminary proposal addressing each of the following sections.
Every sub-point must be addressed in at least one complete sentence, with a max of three sentences.

  1. Personal Goals

    • What do you hope to make? (Go beyond restating the course's learning outcomes or "to learn Figma." Describe your personal ambitions for this project.)
    • Why does this project matter to you?

  2. Content & Experience Format

    • Topic: Primary skeuomorph?
    • Why did you choose this skeuomorph?

    • Identify three secondary skeuomorphs.
    • Why these skeuomorphs?

    • UX Pattern: What experience are you designing? (e.g., a book, game, map; see more examples on the main brief p2: Skeuomorphic Echoes)
    • Why did you choose this format?

    • Media: What types of media will you use? (e.g., text-based, image-heavy, multimedia A/V, branding, characters, dialog, maps, diagrams, illustrations, photography, icons)
    • Why did you choose this media approach?

  3. Research Sources

    • Where will you source your textual content?
    • Where will you source your visual content?
    • Why are these sources appropriate for your project?

  4. Inspiration Sites (Minimum 3)

    • Provide URLs of at least three live projects that inspire your project.
      Need ideas? Try: Awwwards, Game UI Database, or even the app stores: Google, Apple, Steam.
    • For each site, explain what about it inspires you — consider its content, presentation, functionality, or mission.

  5. Form Factor

    • Viewport: Which primary viewport are you designing for?
      • Phone (320–640 px)
      • Tablet (640–960 px)
      • Desktop (960 px+)
    • Why?

    • Format: Website or native app?
    • Why?

    • Input mode: What is the primary input method?
      • Direct (keyboard, game controller, remote control)
      • Fine (mouse, stylus)
      • Coarse (finger, touch, gaze)
    • Why?

    • Location/context of use: Where will users encounter your project?
      • Home, work, school, kiosk (wayfinding, point of purchase, informational)
    • Why?

  6. Project Title

    • What is the working title for your project?
